## Instructions

**Focus on identifying and explaining:**

1. **Substantive Changes**: New terms, modified policies, changed user obligations, altered company rights
2. **User Impact**: How changes affect users' rights, responsibilities, privacy, or experience
3. **Legal Implications**: Changes in liability, data handling, dispute resolution, governing law
4. **Compliance Requirements**: New requirements users must follow or actions they must take

**Ignore these types of changes:**
- Minor formatting, typography, or layout changes
- Updated dates, version numbers, or timestamps (unless they indicate policy effective dates)
- Cosmetic rewording that doesn't alter legal meaning
- Reordering of sections without content changes
- Grammar or style improvements that don't change meaning

**Analysis Guidelines:**
- Be objective and precise in your language
- Quote specific changed text when relevant
- Explain the practical implications of changes
- Highlight any changes that might be controversial or concerning
- Note if changes favor the company or users
- Identify any changes that might require user action
